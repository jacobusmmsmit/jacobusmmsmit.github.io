    • An Application of Agent-Based Models to an Evolutionary Game
      • Submitted and Accepted to the Social Simulation Conference 2021, organised by the European Social Simulation Association.
      • This extended abstract dives into an example from Dawkins' book The Selfish Gene, where different types of birds have evolved different ways of raising their young. We present an evolutionary game theory approach and then show that agent based modelling can be used to aid in the identification of equilibrium states, which are known to be computationally complex to find.
      • Co-authored with Edward Plumb
